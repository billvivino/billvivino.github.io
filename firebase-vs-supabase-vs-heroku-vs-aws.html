Firebase vs Supabase vs AWS vs Heroku

Choose the best backend for your MVP in 2026 by matching the platform to your data, delivery, compliance, cost, and team constraints.

The quick decision shortcuts

  • Firebase: fastest to ship, cost surprises come from reads, listeners, and bandwidth.
  • Supabase: feels cheaper early, surprises shift to compute, storage, and realtime scale.
  • Heroku: convenience premium, costs multiply with always-on dynos and environments.
  • AWS: highest flexibility, you pay for control and operational overhead from day one.

Backend choice FAQ

What is the best backend for an MVP in 2026?

There is no universal winner. Firebase favors speed and managed realtime features, Supabase favors relational data and SQL, AWS favors compliance and control, and Heroku favors straightforward deployment.

Is Firebase better than Supabase for startups?

Firebase is often faster when a managed mobile stack and realtime behavior matter. Supabase is often stronger when relational modeling, reporting, and data portability matter early.

When should an MVP use AWS?

AWS becomes a stronger choice when compliance, infrastructure control, multiple environments, or long-term flexibility outweigh the fastest possible initial delivery.
