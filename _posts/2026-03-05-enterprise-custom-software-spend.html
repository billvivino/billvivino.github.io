The $5T+ IT Spend Reality in 2026

Gartner and IDC forecasts show global IT spending reaching $6.15 trillion in 2026, up 10.8% year-over-year. Software alone grows at 15.2% to $1.43 trillion.
In the US, tech spend hits $2.9 trillion (8.3% growth). AI infrastructure: $230 billion. Digital transformation budgets: double-digit increases across mid-market and enterprise.

“Enterprises are no longer satisfied with rigid SaaS platforms. They want custom software that fits their exact processes — and they’re willing to pay for it.”

Where the Money Is Actually Going

The $213 billion custom software services market (growing to $1.28 trillion by 2033) is not evenly distributed. Here’s where the real spend lives:

  • Cloud migrations & modernization — 40–50% of DX budgets
  • AI & machine learning integrations — fastest-growing segment
  • ERP & CRM customizations — legacy systems still dominate Fortune 500
  • Custom internal tools & dashboards — mid-market efficiency plays
  • Secure, compliant platforms — health, finance, government verticals

These are not $5k no-code MVPs. These are $100k–$1M+ projects with long-term retainers.

Why Off-the-Shelf Isn’t Cutting It Anymore

SaaS promised plug-and-play. Instead it delivered:

  • Feature bloat
  • High licensing costs
  • Integration nightmares
  • Inability to differentiate

Enterprises and growing mid-market companies are now investing in custom software to gain competitive edge, reduce vendor lock-in, and own their data/processes.
