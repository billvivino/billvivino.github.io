Founder context

Why Firebase Costs Surprise Founders

Firebase pricing is not just based on users. It depends on how often your app reads data, writes data, downloads data, and runs backend functions. An app with 5,000 daily users can be cheap or expensive depending on how the screens, listeners, queries, and functions are designed.

Firebase service Cost driver Best for Main risk
Firestore Reads, writes, deletes, storage, egress Structured app data, SaaS, dashboards Read amplification
Realtime Database Stored GB and downloaded GB Presence, simple sync, live state Bandwidth-heavy data sync
Cloud Functions Invocations, compute, outbound networking Backend workflows, automation, integrations Function fan-out

Scenario 1: Small App / MVP

Founder prototype, internal tool, small marketplace, or early mobile app.

Daily active users100-1,000
Firestore reads/day10K-100K
Firestore writes/day1K-20K
Functions/month50K-500K
Risk levelLow

Watch dashboard pages that read entire collections and functions that trigger once per document instead of once per workflow.

Scenario 2: Mid-Scale Product

Funded startup, SaaS dashboard, marketplace, or customer-facing mobile app.

Daily active users5,000-50,000
Firestore reads/day500K-5M
Firestore writes/day50K-500K
Functions/month1M-20M
Risk levelMedium

At this stage, Firestore read patterns matter more than raw user count. A page that reads 200 documents per load can compound quickly.

Scenario 3: Enterprise / High-Volume

Logistics, healthcare workflow, real-time operations, or large B2B SaaS.

Daily active users100,000+
Firestore reads/day10M-100M+
Firestore writes/day1M-20M+
Functions/month50M+
Risk levelHigh

Enterprise costs usually come from architecture that was fine at MVP scale but expensive at production scale.
