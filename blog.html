Behind the Build: NASA WeatherSats AR

Published: April 20, 2025

Back in 2022 I received one of those dream e‑mails: NASA’s Visitor Center wanted help turning their WeatherSats educational exhibit into an augmented‑reality app. They needed someone who could translate flight‑hardware CAD models into smooth, cross‑platform mobile experiences—and do it fast.

I jumped in as the project’s contract Unity developer. My first task was to wrangle huge, high‑detail satellite models into smartphone‑friendly assets. That meant aggressive mesh optimisation, custom shaders for solar‑panel glare, and an animation pipeline that could show everything from orbital deployment to instrument calibration without melting a GPU.

Once performance was locked, the fun began: building interactive timelines and data overlays so students could walk around a virtual GOES satellite, tap a sensor, and see live weather imagery stream onto the screen—all inside their school’s gym or their own backyard.

Unity’s cross‑platform tool‑set let us ship to iOS, Android, and a kiosk‑grade Windows build from the same project. The result? A pocket‑sized museum piece that now accompanies NASA outreach teams across the country.

I still get a kick out of watching kids orbit an AR satellite in the exhibit hall, forget it’s virtual, and ask, “How did NASA fit that in here?”

Have an AR or 3‑D project you need to land? Let’s talk. Whether it’s aerospace visualisation, interactive training, or a marketing demo, I can help you bring complex concepts to life on the devices your audience already owns.
